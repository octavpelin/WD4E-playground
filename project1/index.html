Bucharest - a city of landmarks

A mosaic of architectures and times

Bucharest was first mentioned in documents in 1459. It became the capital of Romania in 1862 and it nowadays stands as the most populated city in Romania, with 1.74 million inhabitants. It has a rich architecture, with mixes of historical (mostly Eclectic, but also Neoclassical and Art Noveau), interbellum (Bauhaus, Art Deco and Romanian Revival Architecture), socialist era and modern styles.

In the period between the two World Wars, the city's elegant architecture and the sophistication of its elite earned Bucharest the nicknames of Little Paris or Paris of the East.
